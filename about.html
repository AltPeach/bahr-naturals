A Journey of Heritage & Heart

From the banks of the Nile to the heart of Canada

Meet Ester Simon

"Beauty traditions are not just routines - they are connections to our ancestors, our land, and our spirit."

Born along the banks of the White Nile in South Sudan, I grew up surrounded by the ancient beauty wisdom of my grandmothers. From childhood, I watched as the women in my family crafted their own skincare remedies using shea butter, natural clays, and botanical oils that had nourished our people for generations.

When circumstances brought my family to Canada, I carried these precious traditions with me like seeds waiting to bloom in new soil. The transition wasn't easy - adapting to a new climate, culture, and way of life while trying to preserve the essence of who we were.

It was during those early years in Canada that I discovered how much my new community hungered for authentic, natural skincare. Friends and neighbors began asking about the beautiful, fragrant soaps I made using my grandmother's recipes. They were amazed by the gentle power of shea butter and the nourishing qualities of traditional ingredients.

Bahr Naturals was born from this intersection - honoring the wisdom of South Sudanese beauty traditions while embracing the quality and craftsmanship standards of my new Canadian home. Each bar of soap is a bridge between two worlds, carrying forward ancient knowledge while meeting modern needs.

Honoring South Sudanese Beauty Traditions

South Sudanese women have long understood the power of natural ingredients to nourish and protect the skin. Our ancestors used shea butter not just as a moisturizer, but as a sacred ritual of self-care and community bonding.

Shea Butter Ceremonies

Communal preparation of shea butter was a time for women to gather, share stories, and pass down beauty wisdom through generations.

Natural Clay Treatments

River clays were used for detoxifying face and body treatments, understanding the earth's power to heal and purify.

Botanical Knowledge

Deep understanding of which plants, oils, and natural materials could soothe, heal, and beautify the skin.
